Logarithimic Differentiation

Logarithmic differentiation is a technique used to differentiate functions that are products or quotients of variables raised to variable powers. It is particularly useful when dealing with functions of the form $y = f(x)^{g(x)}$, where both $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are functions of $x$. By taking the natural logarithm of both sides, we can simplify the differentiation process.

Problem 1:

Differentiate the function $y = x^x$ with respect to $x$.

Solution:

Given the function $y = x^x$.

Since both the base and the exponent involve the variable $x$, we use logarithmic differentiation.

Step 1: Take the natural logarithm of both sides of the equation.

$$ \ln y = \ln(x^x) $$

Step 2: Apply the logarithm property $\ln(a^b) = b \ln a$ to the right side.

$$ \ln y = x \ln x $$

Step 3: Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$. The left side, $\ln y$, requires implicit differentiation. The right side, $x \ln x$, requires the product rule, where $(uv)' = u'v + uv'$. Let $u=x$ and $v=\ln x$.

$$ \frac{d}{dx}(\ln y) = \frac{d}{dx}(x \ln x) $$
$$ \frac{1}{y} \cdot \frac{dy}{dx} = \left(\frac{d}{dx}(x)\right) \ln x + x \left(\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x)\right) $$
$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = (1) \cdot \ln x + x \cdot \left(\frac{1}{x}\right) $$
$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \ln x + 1 $$

Step 4: Solve for $\frac{dy}{dx}$ by multiplying both sides by $y$.

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = y (\ln x + 1) $$

Step 5: Substitute the original expression for $y$ (which is $y=x^x$) back into the equation.

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = x^x (\ln x + 1) $$

Thus, the derivative of $y=x^x$ is $x^x(1 + \ln x)$.

Problem 2:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = \ln(x^2 + 1)^{\sin x}$.

Solution:

Let $y = \ln(x^2 + 1)^{\sin x}$. Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

$$ \ln y = \sin x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) $$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \cos x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) + \sin x \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} $$

Multiply both sides by $y$ and substitute $y = (x^2 + 1)^{\sin x}$:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = (x^2 + 1)^{\sin x} \left[ \cos x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) + \sin x \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} \right] $$

Problem 3:

Differentiate the function $y = (x^2 + 1)^{\tan x}$ with respect to $x$.

Solution:

Let $y = (x^2 + 1)^{\tan x}$. Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

$$ \ln y = \tan x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) $$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \sec^2 x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) + \tan x \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} $$

Multiply both sides by $y$ and substitute $y = (x^2 + 1)^{\tan x}$:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = (x^2 + 1)^{\tan x} \left[ \sec^2 x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) + \tan x \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} \right] $$

Problem 4:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = \frac{(x^2 + 3)^5}{(2x - 1)^4}$.

Solution:

Let $y = \frac{(x^2 + 3)^5}{(2x - 1)^4}$. Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

$$ \ln y = 5 \ln(x^2 + 3) - 4 \ln(2x - 1) $$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = 5 \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 3} - 4 \cdot \frac{2}{2x - 1} $$
$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{10x}{x^2 + 3} - \frac{8}{2x - 1} $$

Multiply both sides by $y$:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{(x^2 + 3)^5}{(2x - 1)^4} \left( \frac{10x}{x^2 + 3} - \frac{8}{2x - 1} \right) $$

Problem 5:

Differentiate $y = x^2 \sqrt{x+1} \, e^{3x}$ with respect to $x$.

Solution:

Let $y = x^2 \sqrt{x+1} \, e^{3x}$. Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

$$ \ln y = 2\ln x + \frac{1}{2}\ln(x+1) + 3x $$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{2}{x} + \frac{1}{2(x+1)} + 3 $$

Multiply both sides by $y$:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = x^2 \sqrt{x+1} \, e^{3x} \left( \frac{2}{x} + \frac{1}{2(x+1)} + 3 \right) $$

Problem 6:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = (x^{\sin x})^{\cos x}$.

Solution:

Let $y = (x^{\sin x})^{\cos x}$. Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

$$ \ln y = \cos x \cdot \ln(x^{\sin x}) = \cos x \cdot \sin x \cdot \ln x $$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}[\cos x \cdot \sin x \cdot \ln x] $$

Let $u = \cos x$, $v = \sin x$, $w = \ln x$. Use the product rule:

$$ \frac{d}{dx}(uvw) = u'vw + uv'w + uvw' $$
$$ u' = -\sin x,\quad v' = \cos x,\quad w' = \frac{1}{x} $$
$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = (-\sin x)\sin x \ln x + \cos x \cos x \ln x + \cos x \sin x \cdot \frac{1}{x} $$
$$ = -\sin^2 x \ln x + \cos^2 x \ln x + \frac{\cos x \sin x}{x} $$

Multiply both sides by $y$:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = (x^{\sin x})^{\cos x} \left[ (\cos^2 x - \sin^2 x)\ln x + \frac{\cos x \sin x}{x} \right] $$

Problem 8:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = e^{x \ln x}$.

Solution:

Rewrite $y = e^{x \ln x} = x^x$.

We have already differentiated $y = x^x$ in Problem 1, but let's show the steps:

$$ \ln y = x \ln x $$
$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \ln x + 1 $$
$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = x^x (\ln x + 1) $$

Problem 9:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = \ln(x^2 + 1)^{\sin x}$.

Solution:

Let $y = \ln(x^2 + 1)^{\sin x}$. Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

$$ \ln y = \sin x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) $$

Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \cos x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) + \sin x \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} $$

Multiply both sides by $y$ and substitute $y = (x^2 + 1)^{\sin x}$:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = (x^2 + 1)^{\sin x} \left[ \cos x \cdot \ln(x^2 + 1) + \sin x \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} \right] $$

Problem 10:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = \ln(\sin(\ln x))$.

Solution:

Let $y = \ln(\sin(\ln x))$.

By the chain rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sin(\ln x)} \cdot \cos(\ln x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} $$
$$ = \frac{\cos(\ln x)}{x \sin(\ln x)} $$

Logarithimic Integration

Logarithmic integration is a technique used to evaluate integrals that involve logarithmic functions. It is particularly useful when dealing with integrals of the form $\int \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \, dx$, where $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are functions of $x$. By using integration by parts or substitution, we can simplify the integral and find its value.

Problem 1:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx$.

Solution:

We can use the property of logarithmic integration:

$$ \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \ln |x| + C $$

Problem 2:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} \, dx$.

Solution:

Let $u = x^2 + 1$, so $du = 2x\,dx$.

$$ \int \frac{2x}{x^2 + 1} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln|u| + C = \ln|x^2 + 1| + C $$

Problem 3:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{3x+2} \, dx$.

Solution:

Let $u = 3x + 2$, so $du = 3\,dx$, $dx = \frac{du}{3}$.

$$ \int \frac{1}{3x+2} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{3} = \frac{1}{3} \ln|u| + C = \frac{1}{3} \ln|3x+2| + C $$

Problem 4:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{5}{2x-7} \, dx$.

Solution:

Let $u = 2x - 7$, so $du = 2\,dx$, $dx = \frac{du}{2}$.

$$ \int \frac{5}{2x-7} \, dx = 5 \int \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{2} = \frac{5}{2} \ln|u| + C = \frac{5}{2} \ln|2x-7| + C $$

Problem 5:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x^2-4} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2-4 = (x-2)(x+2)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{1}{x^2-4} = \frac{A}{x-2} + \frac{B}{x+2} $$

Solve for $A$ and $B$:

$$ 1 = A(x+2) + B(x-2) $$

Let $x=2$: $1 = A(4) \implies A = \frac{1}{4}$
Let $x=-2$: $1 = B(-4) \implies B = -\frac{1}{4}$

$$ \int \frac{1}{x^2-4} dx = \frac{1}{4} \int \frac{1}{x-2} dx - \frac{1}{4} \int \frac{1}{x+2} dx $$
$$ = \frac{1}{4} \ln|x-2| - \frac{1}{4} \ln|x+2| + C $$

Problem 6:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{3x+5}{x^2+5x+6} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2+5x+6 = (x+2)(x+3)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{3x+5}{(x+2)(x+3)} = \frac{A}{x+2} + \frac{B}{x+3} $$

Multiply both sides by $(x+2)(x+3)$:

$$ 3x+5 = A(x+3) + B(x+2) $$

Let $x=-3$: $3(-3)+5 = A(0) + B(-1) \implies -9+5 = -B \implies B = 4$
Let $x=-2$: $3(-2)+5 = A(1) + B(0) \implies -6+5 = A \implies A = -1$

$$ \int \frac{3x+5}{x^2+5x+6} dx = \int \frac{-1}{x+2} dx + \int \frac{4}{x+3} dx $$
$$ = -\ln|x+2| + 4\ln|x+3| + C $$

Problem 7:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2+x = x(x+1)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{2x+1}{x(x+1)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x+1} $$

Multiply both sides by $x(x+1)$:

$$ 2x+1 = A(x+1) + Bx $$

Let $x=0$: $1 = A(1) \implies A=1$
Let $x=-1$: $2(-1)+1 = B(-1) \implies -2+1 = -B \implies B=1$

$$ \int \frac{2x+1}{x^2+x} dx = \int \frac{1}{x} dx + \int \frac{1}{x+1} dx $$
$$ = \ln|x| + \ln|x+1| + C $$

Problem 8:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{4x-1}{2x^2-x} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $2x^2-x = x(2x-1)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{4x-1}{x(2x-1)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{2x-1} $$

Multiply both sides by $x(2x-1)$:

$$ 4x-1 = A(2x-1) + Bx $$

Let $x=0$: $-1 = A(-1) \implies A=1$
Let $x=\frac{1}{2}$: $4(\frac{1}{2})-1 = B(\frac{1}{2}) \implies 2-1 = \frac{B}{2} \implies B=2$

$$ \int \frac{4x-1}{2x^2-x} dx = \int \frac{1}{x} dx + \int \frac{2}{2x-1} dx $$
$$ = \ln|x| + \int \frac{2}{2x-1} dx $$

Let $u=2x-1$, $du=2dx$, so $dx = du/2$:

$$ \int \frac{2}{2x-1} dx = \int \frac{2}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{2} = \int \frac{1}{u} du = \ln|u| + C = \ln|2x-1| + C $$
$$ \int \frac{4x-1}{2x^2-x} dx = \ln|x| + \ln|2x-1| + C $$

Problem 9:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x^2+4x+3} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2+4x+3 = (x+1)(x+3)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{1}{(x+1)(x+3)} = \frac{A}{x+1} + \frac{B}{x+3} $$

Multiply both sides by $(x+1)(x+3)$:

$$ 1 = A(x+3) + B(x+1) $$

Let $x=-3$: $1 = A(0) + B(-2) \implies B = -\frac{1}{2}$
Let $x=-1$: $1 = A(2) + B(0) \implies A = \frac{1}{2}$

$$ \int \frac{1}{x^2+4x+3} dx = \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{x+1} dx - \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{x+3} dx $$
$$ = \frac{1}{2} \ln|x+1| - \frac{1}{2} \ln|x+3| + C $$

Problem 10:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{2x+3}{x^2+3x+2} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2+3x+2 = (x+1)(x+2)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{2x+3}{(x+1)(x+2)} = \frac{A}{x+1} + \frac{B}{x+2} $$

Multiply both sides by $(x+1)(x+2)$:

$$ 2x+3 = A(x+2) + B(x+1) $$

Let $x=-2$: $2(-2)+3 = A(0) + B(-1) \implies -4+3 = -B \implies B=1$
Let $x=-1$: $2(-1)+3 = A(1) + B(0) \implies -2+3 = A \implies A=1$

$$ \int \frac{2x+3}{x^2+3x+2} dx = \int \frac{1}{x+1} dx + \int \frac{1}{x+2} dx $$
$$ = \ln|x+1| + \ln|x+2| + C $$

Problem 11:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x^2-2x} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2-2x = x(x-2)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{1}{x(x-2)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x-2} $$

Multiply both sides by $x(x-2)$:

$$ 1 = A(x-2) + Bx $$

Let $x=0$: $1 = A(-2) \implies A = -\frac{1}{2}$
Let $x=2$: $1 = B(2) \implies B = \frac{1}{2}$

$$ \int \frac{1}{x^2-2x} dx = -\frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{x} dx + \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{x-2} dx $$
$$ = -\frac{1}{2} \ln|x| + \frac{1}{2} \ln|x-2| + C $$

Problem 12:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{3x+2}{x^2+2x} \, dx$.

Solution:

Factor denominator: $x^2+2x = x(x+2)$. Use partial fractions:

$$ \frac{3x+2}{x(x+2)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x+2} $$

Multiply both sides by $x(x+2)$:

$$ 3x+2 = A(x+2) + Bx $$

Let $x=0$: $2 = A(2) \implies A=1$
Let $x=-2$: $3(-2)+2 = B(-2) \implies -6+2 = -2B \implies B=2$

$$ \int \frac{3x+2}{x^2+2x} dx = \int \frac{1}{x} dx + \int \frac{2}{x+2} dx $$
$$ = \ln|x| + 2\ln|x+2| + C $$

Problem 13:

Evaluate the definite integral $\displaystyle \int_{1}^{e} \frac{1}{x} \, dx$.

Solution:

The antiderivative is $\ln|x|$. Thus,

$$ \int_{1}^{e} \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \left[ \ln|x| \right]_{1}^{e} = \ln e - \ln 1 = 1 - 0 = 1 $$

Problem 14:

Evaluate the definite integral $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{1} \frac{2}{x+2} \, dx$.

Solution:

Let $u = x+2$, $du = dx$. The antiderivative is $2\ln|x+2|$.

$$ \int_{0}^{1} \frac{2}{x+2} \, dx = \left[ 2\ln|x+2| \right]_{0}^{1} = 2\ln 3 - 2\ln 2 = 2\ln\left(\frac{3}{2}\right) $$

Problem 15:

Evaluate the definite integral $\displaystyle \int_{1}^{2} \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx$.

Solution:

The antiderivative is $-\frac{1}{x}$.

$$ \int_{1}^{2} \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx = \left[ -\frac{1}{x} \right]_{1}^{2} = -\frac{1}{2} - (-1) = 1 - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2} $$

Derivatives of Inverse Trig Functions

The derivatives of inverse trigonometric functions are important in calculus and have various applications in mathematics and physics. Below are some problems and solutions related to the derivatives of inverse trigonometric functions for your practice.

Problem 1:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \arcsin(2x)$.

Solution:

Let $y = \arcsin(2x)$. By the chain rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(2x)^2}} \cdot 2 = \frac{2}{\sqrt{1-4x^2}} $$

Problem 2:

Differentiate $y = x \arctan x$ with respect to $x$.

Solution:

Use the product rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \arctan x + x \cdot \frac{1}{1+x^2} $$

So,

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \arctan x + \frac{x}{1+x^2} $$

Problem 3:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \arccos(\sqrt{x})$ for $0 < x < 1$.

Solution:

Let $y = \arccos(\sqrt{x})$. By the chain rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(\sqrt{x})^2}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}\sqrt{1-x}} $$

Problem 4:

Differentiate $y = \arctan\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$ for $x > 0$.

Solution:

Let $y = \arctan\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$. By the chain rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{1+\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^2} \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{x^2}\right) = \frac{-1}{x^2 + 1} $$

Problem 5:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \left[ x^2 \arcsin x \right]$.

Solution:

Use the product rule:

$$ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ x^2 \arcsin x \right] = 2x \arcsin x + x^2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} $$

Problem 6:

Differentiate $y = \arccos(3x^2)$ for $|x| < \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$.

Solution:

Let $y = \arccos(3x^2)$. By the chain rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(3x^2)^2}} \cdot 6x = -\frac{6x}{\sqrt{1-9x^4}} $$

Problem 7:

Find $\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \arctan(e^x)$.

Solution:

Let $y = \arctan(e^x)$. By the chain rule:

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{1+(e^x)^2} \cdot e^x = \frac{e^x}{1+e^{2x}} $$

Integrals Involving Inverse Trig Functions

The integrals involving inverse trigonometric functions are important in calculus and have various applications in mathematics and physics. Below are some problems and solutions related to the integrals involving inverse trigonometric functions for your practice.

Problem 1:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx$.

Solution:

This is a standard integral involving the inverse sine function:

$$ \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx = \arcsin x + C $$

Problem 2:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{1+x^2} \, dx$.

Solution:

This is a standard integral involving the inverse tangent function:

$$ \int \frac{1}{1+x^2} \, dx = \arctan x + C $$

Problem 3:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{|x|\sqrt{x^2-1}} \, dx$ for $|x| > 1$.

Solution:

This is a standard integral involving the inverse secant function:

$$ \int \frac{1}{|x|\sqrt{x^2-1}} \, dx = \arcsec |x| + C $$

Problem 4:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{2}{4 + x^2} \, dx$.

Solution:

Rewrite $4 + x^2 = 2^2 + x^2$ and use the formula $\int \frac{1}{a^2 + x^2} dx = \frac{1}{a} \arctan\left(\frac{x}{a}\right) + C$:

$$ \int \frac{2}{4 + x^2} dx = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C = \arctan\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C $$

Problem 5:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int_0^{1/2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dx$.

Solution:

The antiderivative is $\arcsin x$. Thus,

$$ \int_0^{1/2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} dx = \arcsin\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) - \arcsin(0) = \frac{\pi}{6} - 0 = \frac{\pi}{6} $$

Problem 6:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{9 - x^2}} \, dx$.

Solution:

Let $a = 3$, so use $\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}} dx = \arcsin\left(\frac{x}{a}\right) + C$:

$$ \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{9 - x^2}} dx = \arcsin\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) + C $$

Problem 7:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x^2 + 6x + 10} \, dx$.

Solution:

Complete the square: $x^2 + 6x + 10 = (x+3)^2 + 1$.

$$ \int \frac{1}{(x+3)^2 + 1} dx = \arctan(x+3) + C $$

Problem 8:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4x - x^2}} \, dx$ for $0 < x < 4$.

Solution:

Complete the square: $4x - x^2 = -(x^2 - 4x) = -(x^2 - 4x + 4 - 4) = -[(x-2)^2 - 4] = 4 - (x-2)^2$.

$$ \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4x - x^2}} dx = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 - (x-2)^2}} dx $$

Let $u = x-2$, $du = dx$:

$$ = \arcsin\left(\frac{x-2}{2}\right) + C $$

Problem 9:

Evaluate the integral $\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x^2 - 4x + 5} \, dx$.

Solution:

Complete the square: $x^2 - 4x + 5 = (x-2)^2 + 1$.

$$ \int \frac{1}{(x-2)^2 + 1} dx = \arctan(x-2) + C $$

Problem 10:

Evaluate the definite integral $\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1 + x^2} \, dx$.

Solution:

The antiderivative is $\arctan x$:

$$ \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1 + x^2} dx = \arctan(1) - \arctan(0) = \frac{\pi}{4} - 0 = \frac{\pi}{4} $$

Problem 11:

Evaluate the definite integral $\displaystyle \int_1^2 \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 - x^2}} \, dx$.

Solution:

The antiderivative is $\arcsin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$:

$$ \int_1^2 \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 - x^2}} dx = \arcsin(1) - \arcsin\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{6} = \frac{\pi}{3} $$
